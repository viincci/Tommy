About

Tommy, who was born in Johannesburg in 1963, spent 1968–1979 studying at the Kutlwanong school for the deaf. While still in school, he started drawing, and after graduating from high school, he pursued his art studies at the Johannesburg Art Foundation and FUBA. He started exhibiting after receiving a grant from Sanlam in 1985. One of his paintings was displayed at a global exhibition in Monte Carlo in 1986, and he was awarded a distinction medal in the Volkskas Atelier exhibition in 1987. He was chosen for the Cape Town Triennale shortly after that. Additionally, he received the Standard Bank Young Artist of the Year honor. Collectors admire his art, and significant public and private collections can be found all around South Africa.

Tommy Motswai's workplace is buzzing with activity, with people going about their daily lives and laughing, chatting, and yelling as automobiles and trains whiz by. However, Tommy is deaf from birth, so he is unable to hear any of this. What he observes is what matters, and when he captures it on paper, it is a testament to the happiness, excitement, and pleasure he experiences in his surroundings.

Motswai began working with The Artists' Press in 1993, and since then, the studio has created a large number of editions. His colorful working style is well suited to lithography, and it is always a delight to have him in the studio. His visual perception is extraordinarily sharp, and he manipulates the intricate multiple-color overlays naturally, as if he had always done so.

He deliberately makes an effort to document significant events and moments in life and pays close attention to every little detail as he sees it. He frequently uses humor and amicable interactions in his writing. He only captures the positive and upbeat aspects of his environment, leaving out all the disappointments and hopelessness.
